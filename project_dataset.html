Expenditures on healthcare, by country

Provided by the World Health Organization

The Data

Source data at WHO.int
You can find the data in my GitHub repository here

The dataset describes the different expenditures on healthcare by country and by year, spanning from 1995 to 2012. Some of the breakdowns of expenditures include: total expenditure on healthcare as a percentage of GDP, general government expenditure on health as a percentage of total government spending, and out-of-pocket expenditure as a percentage of total health spending. With the exception of country and year, all of the fields are represented as percentages.

Why this Dataset

Healthcare, specifically healthcare spending, seems to be at the center of every political debate. Of course, numbers that are presented in a vacuum are, generally, useless. The visualizations in this project are meant to provide some context to how different countries spend money on healthcare relative to their peers. When presented in context, the data may present some insights into our stance in the United States on healthcare and help us decide the direction in which we want to take our healthcare policies.
